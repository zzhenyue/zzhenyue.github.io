Hi, I'm Jack Chao.

I study how people, ideas, and technologies connect — and how we can use AI to accelerate scientific discovery.

About Me

I am a computational social scientist working at the intersection of scholarly communication, bibliometrics, and AI-for-science tooling. My research spans large-scale analysis of publication and citation networks, topic emergence detection, and building machine learning systems that help researchers navigate scientific literature at scale.

Recent work includes clustering millions of scholarly records across heterogeneous data sources (Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex, PubMed, Dimensions), modeling the uptake of advanced AI tools (e.g., protein structure prediction systems) across research communities, and designing recommendation systems that infer emerging collaborations and material science trajectories.

Broadly, I'm interested in how data traces of science can feed back into better tools, better decisions, and faster discovery.
